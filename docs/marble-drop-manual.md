# Marble Drop Original Game Manual Reference

Source: https://www.maxlaumeister.com/marble-drop/in-game-help/

## Contraptions

### Diverter
Diverters allow marbles to change tracks. Each time the marble passes through a diverter, the direction of the diverter will change. Thus, the next time a marble passes through it will go a different way.
- **Points**: 25

### Brake
A brake is a component used to slow a marble down as the poor defenseless marble slides into a vertical free fall. The brake appears as a vertical pipe with several pairs of metal "wings" which slow the speed of the marble, and make clackity-clack sounds as they do so.
- **Points**: 5

### Lift
A lift is a device that moves a marble vertically in a puzzle. It resembles a conveyer belt with scoops that pick up and relocate marbles to a different track. Lifts can be various lengths. Puzzles may contain more than one lift.

### Spring
A spring appears as an archaic loaded crossbow. If a marble lands on a spring, it will be launched to a receiving basket located elsewhere in the puzzle.
- **Points**: 10

### Cannon
A cannon fires a marble like a cannonball into a receiving basket located elsewhere in puzzle. The marble will roll into the cannon barrel, which will then be tilted upward. When another marble hits the trigger (usually located on a different track), the cannon fires.
- **Points**: 200

### Heater
A heater is a jet of flame that a marble can roll through. The first time a marble rolls through a heater, it becomes "hot" and turns a copper color. If the marble rolls through a heater a second time, it will melt and be destroyed.
- **Points**: 25

### Freezer
The opposite of a heater, the freezer appears as a glass dome which emits an icy mist. A marble that rolls through a freezer will become "cold" and change color. The second time through a freezer and the marble will shatter and be destroyed.
- **Points**: 25

### Teleporter
There are 2 types of teleporters: senders and receivers. A sending teleporter sends marbles to a receiving teleporter. Sending teleporters' lights are white, non-active receiving teleporters' lights are dark, and active receiving teleporters' lights are yellow. Only one teleporter is active to receive at a time.
- **Points**: 50

### Painter
A marble that passes through a painter changes to the color of the tanks of paint. That is, a green marble passing through a red painter turns red.
**Note**: Steel marbles CANNOT be painted, and will pass through the painter unchanged.

### Destructor
Any marble unfortunate enough to roll into a destructor is destroyed. The destructor may be recognized by its two spring arms with spheres on the ends.

### Spinner
A spinner is really a pinwheel that spins around when rolled over by a marble, creating a nice visual effect.
- **Points**: 25

### Triggered Diverter
A triggered diverter is slightly different from a regular diverter in that it only changes direction when triggered by an electrical impulse.

### Blocker
A blocker blocks a marble's path as it rolls down the track. Blockers can be in a raised (blocked) or lowered (clear) position and are controlled by triggers located on other sections of track.

### Splitter
When a marble rolls into a splitter, it is divided into two identical marbles.

### Merger
The merger combines two marbles into one. A marble rolls into the merger and awaits arrival of a second marble. The merger will now be lit. When the second marble rolls into the merger, a single marble will be released, whose color will be determined by the colors of the marbles involved.

### Glue
Glue is the sticky substance found on the track. A marble that hits the glue is stuck there, until it is knocked free by another marble that takes its place.

### Trigger
A trigger sends an electrical impulse when activated.
- **Points**: 10

### Target
A target is activated when hit by a marble.
- **Points**: 25

## Marble Types

### Colored Marbles
The six standard colors: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Purple
- **Purchase Cost**: 500 points

### Black Marbles
The black marble may be chosen to act as a "transforming" marble, which takes on, chameleon-like, the color of the marble needed to solve the puzzle (matches any exit bin).
- **Purchase Cost**: 1500 points
- **EXCEPTION**: Black marbles CANNOT fill the steel marble slot in an exit bin.

### Steel Marbles
If you find you must "sacrifice" a marble to trip a switch, change a diverter, be destroyed or be caught in an infinite loop, consider purchasing a Steel Marble. Steel marbles only cost 100 points.
- **Purchase Cost**: 100 points
- **Note**: STEEL MARBLES CANNOT BE PAINTED! They will pass right through the painter unchanged.

## Scoring

### Basic Scoring Rules
1. Points are earned each time a component is used by a marble
2. 500 points are awarded for every marble that makes it into the correct exit bin
3. When the puzzle is completed, a large bonus (100-5000 points) is awarded based on efficiency

### Score Breakdown
- **Components Used**: Number of components used to solve the puzzle
- **# Of Marbles Purchased**: The number of marbles needed to buy
- **Efficiency Bonus**: Bonus points for using fewer marbles
