# Marble Drop Level Reference Guide

Source: Extracted from [Lupine.dev](https://lupine.dev/marble-drop/) and [FAQ](https://lupine.dev/marble-drop/marble-drop-faq-derek-kisman.txt)

## Global Art Direction

Every level should feel like a **Leonardo da Vinci sketch-machine**: parchment background, sepia engineering notes, hand-drawn diagrams, brass/copper/silver rails, wood-and-metal traps, visible red trigger wires, little lamps, small pegs, marble baskets, and mechanical gadgets that look both elegant and ridiculous.

Common objects:

- **Funnels / start baskets:** top-mounted brass funnels where the player drops marbles.
- **Exit bins:** small bottom trays with colored strips showing accepted marble colors.
- **Holding tubes:** tall black vertical tubes with side pegs and ring tracks; marbles can loop, pause, or be released.
- **Diverters:** green flipper-like gates that change route after being hit.
- **Switches / buttons:** tiny pegs, red blocks, gear levers, or wire-linked triggers.
- **Tubes / ramps:** grey, bronze, copper, gold, colored rails, spiral tracks, and enclosed pipe sections.
- **Hazards:** saws, burners, destructors, cannons, crossbows, glue blobs, crushers, and timed blockers.
- **Teleporters / receivers:** silver saucer pads with yellow lights.
- **Generators / mergers / splitters / painters:** later-game machinery that creates, combines, recolors, or redirects marbles.

## Level Details

### Level 1: Thales of Miletus
**Layout:** Introductory symmetrical board with two top funnels, one on the upper-left and one on the upper-right. The left and right halves cross diagonally through the center, creating an X-shaped routing puzzle. Each side has a tall vertical holding tube near the edge, with a gold ring around the top and bottom.
**Mechanics:** Green diverters sit on the diagonal metal rails and determine whether marbles descend into side spirals, cross over, or enter the central brass fork. The center contains a golden vertical chute with alternating fins, splitting paths downward. The bottom has several colored exit bins, mainly focused on primary colors.
**Remake notes:** This level teaches the player that routes can cross and that a marble's path changes future marbles. Use it as the tutorial template for gravity rails, diverters, holding tubes, and matching bins.

### Level 2: Tarquinius the Elder
**Layout:** Three top funnels spread left, center, and right. The board is more horizontal than Level 1, with long grey rails crossing from one side to the other. Large spiral descenders sit under the left and right funnels.
**Mechanics:** This level introduces a **target/saw trigger concept**. A marble can hit a target-like spinner that changes route state. The right side includes a vertical tube with several black fins, forcing marbles to descend through a controlled release path. Bottom exits are separated into left, center-left, and right trays.

### Level 3: Priscian
**Layout:** Two main top funnels. The left funnel feeds into a "three-way dropper" style mechanism with branching rails; the right funnel feeds a long looping rail system and a tall right-side holding tube. The center is dense with crossing tracks and curved returns.
**Mechanics:** Introduces more serious **looping and delayed routing**. There is a grey bridge pipe near the lower-left/center and a green diverter in the mid-right. The right-side vertical tube acts as a timing reservoir.

### Level 4: Xenophon
**Layout:** Large symmetrical two-funnel board with tall holding tubes on both far sides. The left and right halves mirror each other with gold rails, grey rails, and two vertical fin-tubes near center-left and center-right.
**Mechanics:** A central pendulum-like element and a horizontal tumbler barrel at the bottom center form the core.

### Level 5: Galileo (Bonus)
**Layout:** Bonus conversion board with two top funnels and a dense set of brightly colored rails: cyan, yellow, and blue. The board is flatter and more abstract than the normal levels, with register-like horizontal rails and many vertical pin markers.
**Mechanics:** Designed around **registers** and bonus scoring. It converts colored marbles into black marbles.

### Level 6: Aristotle
**Layout:** Two top funnels with a complex field of spirals, small holding tubes, green diverters, and red switch wires. There are tall holding tubes on both left and right edges, with a cluster of interactive parts in the center.
**Mechanics:** The level introduces stronger use of **holding tubes** as temporary storage.

### Level 7: Archimedes
**Layout:** Two top funnels positioned upper-left and upper-right, feeding into two broad diagonal rail systems that cross in the lower center. Each side has spring/gear-like blockers connected by red wires.
**Mechanics:** This level introduces **blockers**—mechanical arms or barriers that temporarily stop or release marbles. The red wiring makes cause-and-effect visible.

### Level 8: Euclid
**Layout:** Three top funnels spaced left, center, and right. The left and right sides are built from tall spiral descenders, while the center has a smoother curved rail network. Several small trigger pegs and red wires connect lower-left machinery to mid-right machinery.
**Mechanics:** Uses split routes, switch-linked gates, and several separate exit bins along the bottom.

### Level 9: Eratosthenes
**Layout:** A broad, symmetrical level with a left holding tube, a right holding tube, and a central "array" of target wheels. Two top funnels sit near the upper center/upper right. The lower half has two mirrored exit-bin clusters.
**Mechanics:** Features multiple **resetting targets** or spring wheels arranged in rows.

### Level 10: Polybius
**Layout:** Two top funnels near the upper center, flanked by tall holding tubes on far left and far right. A mirrored set of curved yellow/gold tracks forms a broad U-shape around the lower half.
**Mechanics:** The level uses **multiple triggers linked to dual side tubes**.

### Level 11: Ctesibius
**Layout:** Two top funnels and three vertical holding tubes: left, center, and right. A cannon sits on the left side, while curved tracks and red wiring connect across the entire board. The right side has a tall spiral and a right holding tube.
**Mechanics:** Introduces or emphasizes **cannon-like propulsion** and triggered diverters.

### Level 12: Ma Chun
**Layout:** Two top funnels, one left and one right, with a large open middle. The track network is mostly long, smooth, crossing diagonals and broad spirals on both sides. Bottom bins sit at left, center, and right.
**Mechanics:** The visible notes reference **glue**. Several white glue-like blobs sit along the lower rails.

### Level 13: Hero of Alexandria
**Layout:** Two top funnels feeding into an enclosed network of thick metallic pipes. The board is dominated by long diagonal tubes crossing like an X, with copper and silver pipe textures. Bottom exits are left-center and right-center.
**Mechanics:** Focuses on **enclosed pipe routing**. Green arrows/diverters inside pipes show the current direction.

### Level 14: Speusippus
**Layout:** Two top funnels in the upper-left/center, with a cannon on the far left, two small burners/flames in the center, a horn-like shooter on the upper-right, and a tall right-side holding tube.
**Mechanics:** Strongly hazard-focused. Marbles can be burned by flames, fired/redirected by cannon-like devices.

### Level 15: Democritus
**Layout:** Two top funnels, left and right, with a symmetric-ish basket/crossbow theme. There are curved yellow rails, two target wheels, white glue pads in the center, and two black insect/crossbow figures at bottom corners.
**Mechanics:** Introduces a **basket/crossbow** style mechanism.

### Level 16: Brunelleschi
**Layout:** Two top funnels, one upper-left and one upper-center. The board is sparse and elegant, dominated by colored marbles sitting on white glue pads along a winding yellow rail network. A cluster of green diverters sits in the center.
**Mechanics:** This is a marble-collection/gain puzzle. The goal is to free or route marbles from glue.

### Level 17: Archytas of Tarente
**Layout:** Two top funnels, with a tall central counter-like mechanism and thick copper pipe loops forming a large vertical oval. The right side contains a tumbler barrel with red lights; bottom exits sit under diagonal rails.
**Mechanics:** Introduces a **counter** or pulse-counting mechanism.

### Level 18: Christiaan Huygens (Bonus)
**Layout:** Two top funnels and a highly colored red/blue/white rail network. There are several holding tubes and a large right-side vertical tube. Tracks are mostly red and blue, with white neutral rails crossing between them.
**Mechanics:** Bonus black-marble conversion level.

### Level 19: Philo of Athens
**Layout:** Two top funnels. The left side contains a cannon and tall spiral; the right side has a tall helical route and a crossbow-like catch area. A central pendulum/switch region controls access between sides.
**Mechanics:** The FAQ says the player must repeatedly drop marbles to reset the pendulum to the right side. This is a **timing and pendulum reset** puzzle.

### Level 20: Cato the Elder
**Layout:** Two top funnels near upper center, with mirrored left and right holding tubes. The central area has large crossing copper/silver pipes forming an X. Four burners/flames are placed near important pipe entries.
**Mechanics:** Heavy **flame/heater** puzzle.

### Level 21: Philo of Byzantium
**Layout:** Two top funnels feed a broad field of many small crossbow/wheel targets. The board has multiple bottom bins spread left-to-right, and the route network is a series of shallow arcs.
**Mechanics:** A **crossbow/return** puzzle.

### Level 22: Hipparchus
**Layout:** Two top funnels, two large red helical side loops, several glue pads holding colored marbles, and two central blocker/lever assemblies. Bottom has left and right exit clusters.
**Mechanics:** Glue-held colored marbles can be harvested at the cost of steel marbles.

### Level 23: Shao Ong
**Layout:** Two top funnels, tall left and right holding tubes, and a central pair of vertical fin-tubes. Multiple teleporters and receivers are labeled across the board. There are silver saucer pads with yellow lights on both sides and middle.
**Mechanics:** This is the **teleporter introduction**.

### Level 24: Dionysus Thrax
**Layout:** Three top funnels across the upper area. The board is filled with parallel zig-zag rails, red wiring, timer elements, and small gear blockers. Three bottom exit bins are spread left, center, and right.
**Mechanics:** Introduces **timers** and complex red-wire trigger networks.

### Level 25: Geminus of Rhodes
**Layout:** Two top funnels, a central splitter assembly, multiple receiver pads, and two lower gear-like generators. The board contains wave-shaped rails and several red wire connections.
**Mechanics:** The key object is the **splitter**, which sends one marble into multiple possible paths.

### Level 26: Plato
**Layout:** Two top funnels feed a large circular/oval pipe machine. The center contains multiple green diverters and a red-button central mechanism. Four bottom exits sit under the circular pipe frame.
**Mechanics:** A bonus-unlock/tumbler puzzle with a circular traffic pattern.

### Level 27: Sripati (Bonus)
**Layout:** Three top funnels: left, upper-right, and far-right. The board resembles a tree. A central trunk pipe descends into multiple root-like output branches, while red rails form the upper branches.
**Mechanics:** Bonus black-marble conversion. The central trunk has a vertical fin-tube that meters marbles downward.

### Level 28: Marcus Tiron
**Layout:** Two top funnels, many receiver pads, two marble generators at the top corners, and merger machinery at the lower-right. The board has a central tall spiral and two green diverters.
**Mechanics:** Introduces the **merger**. The merger combines marbles based on a formula.

### Level 29: Pliny the Elder
**Layout:** Four top funnels, arranged left, center-left, center-right, and right. The level is split into two mirrored vertical lane systems with crossing white/copper rails.
**Mechanics:** Uses clean, almost ladder-like routing with green diverters and receivers.

### Level 30: Vitruvius
**Layout:** Two top funnels. A tall left holding tube, a smaller central holding tube, and a right vertical scoring/tube mechanism. The center has long yellow and white diagonal rails crossing from left to right.
**Mechanics:** Focuses on **receiver routing** and elongated path correction.

### Level 31: Ts'ai Lun
**Layout:** Three top funnels: left, center, and right. The center has a three-way dropper under the middle funnel. The lower area is split into left and right pipe clusters with two fin-tubes and curved copper pipes.
**Mechanics:** Uses the **three-way dropper**, tumbler tubes, and timed route changes.

### Level 32: Apollonius Dyskolos
**Layout:** Two top funnels, lots of receiver pads, several flame burners, freezer-like white cylinders, and one tall right-side holding tube. The left side has a large white spiral and a crossbow/launcher at the top.
**Mechanics:** Combines **freezer/heater state** with teleporters.

### Level 33: Belisarius
**Layout:** Two top funnels, a huge crossing pair of dark rails forming an X, several flame burners, a tumbler bonus device near the bottom center, and teleporters/receivers scattered around.
**Mechanics:** Advanced heater/bonus route puzzle.

### Level 34: Apollonius (Bonus)
**Layout:** Three top funnels and three strongly colored lane systems: red left, yellow center, blue right. The board has repeated zig-zag rails, score pegs, and bottom black exit bins.
**Mechanics:** Bonus black-marble conversion with color-coded lane routing.

### Level 35: Isidore of Seville
**Layout:** Three top funnels with a central tree/trunk assembly similar to Level 27 but more complex. The upper half contains a green network of wire-like connections between many small blocker components.
**Mechanics:** Combination-lock style. The top row of blockers operates like a code or access pattern.

### Level 36: Chang Hsu-hsun
**Layout:** Three top funnels. The center is a large ornate pipe sculpture with a looped oval core, multiple teleporters, and a right-side vertical fin-tube. Left and right sides have stacked receiver pads feeding side bins.
**Mechanics:** Teleporter-heavy.

### Level 37: Gerbert d'Aurillac
**Layout:** Four top funnels across the upper edge. The board has a large circular pipe loop around the middle, several green diverters inside, and stacked side receiver pads.
**Mechanics:** A reusable circular route where marbles can be redirected through center diverters.

### Level 38: Pi Cheng
**Layout:** Two top funnels. The center is crowded with vertical fin-tubes, generator capsules, receiver pads, and thick curved pipes. The left side has a small glue/steel-related note, while the right side includes a smaller two-marble loop.
**Mechanics:** Generator-focused.

### Level 39: Gui d'Arezzo
**Layout:** Three top funnels on the upper-right, plus a left-side holding tube. Three painter/generator stations on the upper-right produce red, yellow, and blue marbles. The center has a three-way pipe manifold feeding generator/painter routes.
**Mechanics:** This is a **perpetual motion / painter** level. A marble generator repeatedly creates marbles.

### Level 40: Su Sung
**Layout:** Two top funnels inside a large pyramid-shaped machine. The structure uses thick black, gold, and copper pipes forming an A-frame/pyramid. Bottom exits are spread underneath the pyramid.
**Mechanics:** Fast, chaotic timing puzzle. The FAQ specifically calls it "fast and furious."

### Level 41: Guido di Vigevano
**Layout:** Two top funnels, a three-way dropper under the left funnel, several green diverters, three target wheels, and multiple receiver pads. The right side has a horn/launcher feeding a receiver route.
**Mechanics:** Focuses on controlled speed and receiver routing.

### Level 42: Salvino degli Armati
**Layout:** Two top funnels, a left-side crossing rail network, and a large circular clock/timer loop on the right containing a steel marble. A bonus tumbler sits inside or near the loop, with receiver pads around it.
**Mechanics:** The level includes its own timer: a steel marble loops perpetually.

### Level 43: Albertus Magnus (Bonus)
**Layout:** Bonus level. The screenshot did not render cleanly, so this one should be treated as **needs visual verification**.
**Mechanics:** Black-marble conversion with steel-marble involvement. Yields nine black marbles, requiring nine colored marbles plus two steel marbles.

### Level 44: Leone Alberti
**Layout:** Three top funnels, each feeding a separate compact circular machine: red system on the left, yellow system in the center, blue/silver system on the right. Each machine has a similar looped structure with a receiver pad near the top.
**Mechanics:** Three nearly separated systems, each using teleporters and internal loops.

### Level 45: Timdeharis
**Layout:** Two top funnels on the upper-right and a left-side holding tube. The board includes painter tubes, a merger, a generator/perpetual motion region, and long looping pipes across the center-right.
**Mechanics:** Another innately timed puzzle where time is measured by the number of marbles generated.

### Level 46: Giovanni
**Layout:** Three top funnels with a dense pipe network crossing diagonally through the center. There are several copper ramps, black structural rails, red arrows, receiver pads, and a right-side fin-tube.
**Mechanics:** Uses angled crossing pipes and "take one wrong route and the marble is lost" logic.

### Level 47: Kiddinu
**Layout:** Two top funnels inside another pyramid-shaped machine, similar to Level 40 but more advanced. The center contains stacked circular components and a paired-button/painter assembly. Bottom exits sit under the pyramid legs.
**Mechanics:** Shape-changing pyramid puzzle. Routes appear to reconfigure through central circular switches.

### Level 48: Thabit Ibn Quarra
**Layout:** Two top funnels feeding a large circular/spiral pipe machine. The center contains splitter/merger-like mechanisms, receivers, and a rotating loop. Several exit bins sit along the bottom.
**Mechanics:** Very complex routing with teleporters acting like splitters and mergers.

### Level 49: Gutenberg
**Layout:** Three top funnels with a dense network of vertical fin-tubes, teleporters, generators, and long red paths. The left and right edges have stacked receiver pads; the center has multiple tube columns and domed generator pods.
**Mechanics:** Teleporter/generator overload. Disappearing marbles and confusing route restarts.

### Level 50: Copernicus (The Invisible Level)
**Layout:** Three top funnels and three black exit bins at the bottom, but the actual machine is invisible. The parchment contains Leonardo-style handwritten apology/lore text instead of visible rails.
**Mechanics:** The FAQ notes that this is essentially the same puzzle as Level 8, despite being invisible. The player must solve it by understanding hidden routes.

## Technical Notes

For a true pixel-perfect remake, you'd still want to extract original level data, collision splines, triggers, and component states from the game files.

This document serves as a **visual/mechanical design spec**, not as executable physics data. 

The correct instruction for the remake AI is:
> Recreate each board as a gravity-driven marble contraption using the listed funnels, bins, tracks, switches, tubes, diverters, hazards, teleporters, generators, mergers, splitters, painters, timers, and bonus conversion systems. Preserve the parchment/Leonardo sketch art direction, the relative left-center-right layout, and the main mechanic identity of each level. Do not invent modern sci-fi styling; keep everything Renaissance-mechanical, hand-built, brass/copper/iron, with visible red trigger wiring and handwritten notes.
